How to cook a perfect Biriyani...

Biriyani is more than just a dish; it is an emotion woven with aroma, spice, and tradition. Long-grained basmati rice, tender meat or vegetables, and carefully balanced spices come together in slow-cooked harmony. As the lid is lifted, the fragrant steam carries hints of saffron, ghee, and whole spices, instantly awakening the senses and promising a deeply satisfying experience.

Each bite of biriyani tells a story of patience and craftsmanship. The rice remains fluffy yet flavorful, while the meat melts effortlessly, having absorbed the richness of yogurt, herbs, and spices. Whether enjoyed at a family gathering, a celebration, or a quiet meal, biriyani creates a sense of comfort and joy that lingers long after the last spoonful.

Ingredients:

  • Water
  • Milk
  • Tea leaves or tea bag
  • Sugar (to taste)
  • Ginger or cardamom (optional)

Steps:

  1. Boil water in a pan.
  2. Add tea leaves and let it brew for 1–2 minutes.
  3. Pour in milk and add sugar as required.
  4. Add ginger or cardamom for extra flavor (optional).
  5. Boil for another minute, strain, and serve hot.
